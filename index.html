Game Rules and Explanations

Introduction

Win Condition: Defeat the Elite Four at Indigo Plateau to become the Pokémon Champion.

Lose Condition: If all Pokémon in your party reach 0 HP, it’s Game Over. You can restart from the beginning.

Getting Started: Choose one of four Characters and a Starter Pokémon: select one of five Starter Pokémon to begin your journey in Pallet Town.

Party and Inventory: Your chosen Pokémon is added to your party (up to 6 Pokémon). You also start with a small amount of Gold and basic items (Pokeballs and Potions).

Explore Areas: Navigate through 10 areas, each with unique backgrounds.

Game Rules and Explanations

Areas and Progression

Each area has an increased difficulty to pokemon encountered:

  • Pallet Town
  • Pewter City
  • Cerulean City
  • Celadon City
  • Fuchsia City
  • Saffron City
  • Cinnabar Island
  • Viridian City
  • Vermilion City
  • Indigo Plateau

Use “Return to Last Area” or “Go to Next Area” buttons to move between areas.

Gym Challenges: Each area (except Pallet Town) has a Gym with a Gym Leader. Defeat them to earn a Gym Badge.

Collect all 8 badges to challenge the Elite Four at Indigo Plateau.

Game Rules and Explanations

Pokémon Party and Management

Party Size: You can have up to 6 Pokémon in your party. Extra Pokémon are stored in your Collection.

Party Order: Outside of battle, rearrange your party using the "Switch Pokemon" button on Party Status screen.

HP (Hit Points): Each Pokémon has an HP value representing its health. If a Pokémon’s HP reaches 0, it faints and the next Pokémon in your party takes its place automatically.

Collection: View and manage stored Pokémon at the Pokécenter. Transfer Pokémon between your party and Collection selecting the Pokémon and clicking "Transfer". Must have an open Party Slot to transfer from collection to party

Game Rules and Explanations

Battles

Battles are turn-based and occur when encountering wild Pokémon, battling trainers, or challenging Gym Leaders.

Battle Types

Wild Pokémon Battles: Search for wild Pokémon in an area. Each area has a higher chance of spawning stronger Pokémon..

Trainer Battles: Battle NPC trainers with a randomly selected Pokémon party of up to 5. You cannot catch their Pokémon.

Gym Battles: Fight Gym Leaders with stronger Pokémon parties. Winning earns a Gym Badge.

Elite Four: The final challenge at Indigo Plateau, featuring tough opponents. Beat them to become the Pokémon Champion and claim Victory!

Game Rules and Explanations

Battle Actions

Accept Challenge: Starts a Trainer, Gym, or Elite Four battle.

Attack: Your Pokémon attacks the opponent. The Pokémon will attack for a random damage value based on their Max HP.

Throw Pokéball: Available only in wild Pokémon battles after HP is 0. Catches the Pokémon, adding it to your party (or Collection if your party is full). Cannot capture multiple of the same Pokémon.

Use Potion: Restores 20 HP to your current Pokémon. Can be used at any time during a battle.

Switch Pokémon: Select the next Pokémon in your party to fight.

Run Away: Ends a wild Pokémon battle and returns you to the Area screen. Not available in Trainer, Gym, or Elite Four battles.

Game Rules and Explanations

Battle End

Accept Gym Badge: Claims a badge after defeating a Gym Leader.

Accept Defeat: Available after defeating Trainers or Elite Four Members.

Battle Outcomes

Victory: Defeat all opponent Pokémon (their HP reaches 0).

Defeat: If all your party Pokémon reach 0 HP, it’s Game Over.

Rewards: Gold and a chance of Rare Candy from wild Pokémon.

Rare Candy gives +1 EXP to the current Pokémon. Only useable outside of battle.

EXP: Pokémon gain 1 EXP per battle won.

Game Rules and Explanations

Pokémon Growth

Experience (XP): Pokémon gain XP from battles. Pokémon evolve when they gain enough XP. If Pokémon cannot evolve, they gain +30 HP.

Power: A Pokémon’s strength is based on its HP. Higher HP means better performance in battles.

Poké Center & Poké Mart

Heal Pokémon: Restores all party Pokémon to full HP.

Check Collection: View and transfer Pokémon between your party and Collection.

Buy "Item": Will add 1 of the item selected to Item Bag and deduct the Gold.

Leave: Returns to the Area screen.

Game Rules and Explanations

Wild Pokémon and Rarity

Use “Search for Wild Pokémon” to encounter a wild Pokémon. A random Pokémon will spawn.

Rarity: Pokémon have a rarity value affecting their spawn chance. Rare Pokémon are harder to find.

Catching: Reduce a wild Pokémon’s HP to 0, then use a Pokéball to catch it.

Strategy Tips

Heal Often: Visit Poké Centers and use Potions during battle.

Catch Pokémon: Build a strong team by catching wild Pokémon.

Battle Trainers: Gain XP to strengthen your team.

Evolve Pokémon: Use battles and Rare Candies to evolve Pokémon.
