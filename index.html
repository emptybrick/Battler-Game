Game Rules and Explanations

Introduction

Win Condition: Defeat the Elite Four at Indigo Plateau to become the Pokémon Champion.

Lose Condition: If all Pokémon in your party reach 0 HP, it’s Game Over. You can restart from the beginning.

Getting Started: Choose a Starter Pokémon: select one of four Starter Pokémon to begin your journey in Pallet Town.

Party and Inventory: Your chosen Pokémon is added to your party (up to 6 Pokémon). You also start with a small amount of Gold and basic items (Pokeballs and Potions).

Explore Areas: Navigate through 10 areas, each with unique Pokémon types, trainers, and Gyms.

Game Rules and Explanations

Areas and Progression

Each area has an increased chance to encounter a specific Pokémon type:

  • Pallet Town (Normal)
  • Pewter City (Rock)
  • Cerulean City (Water)
  • Celadon City (Grass)
  • Fuchsia City (Poison)
  • Saffron City (Psychic)
  • Cinnabar Island (Fire)
  • Viridian City (Ground)
  • Vermilion City (Electric)
  • Indigo Plateau (Dragon)

Use “Return to Return to Last Area” or “Go to Next Area Go to Next Area ” buttons to move between areas.

Gym Challenges: Each area (except Pallet Town) has a Gym with a Gym Leader. Defeat them to earn a Gym Badge.

Collect all 8 badges to challenge the Elite Four at Indigo Plateau.

Game Rules and Explanations

Pokémon Party and Management

Party Size: You can have up to 6 Pokémon in your party. Extra Pokémon are stored in your Collection.

Party Order: Outside of battle, rearrange your party using up/down arrows on the Party Status screen.

HP (Hit Points): Each Pokémon has an HP value representing its health. If a Pokémon’s HP reaches 0, it faints and the next Pokémon in your party takes its place automatically.

Collection: View and manage stored Pokémon at the Pokécenter. Transfer Pokémon between your party and Collection using arrows next to each Pokémon.

Game Rules and Explanations

Battles

Battles are turn-based and occur when encountering wild Pokémon, battling trainers, or challenging Gym Leaders/Elite Four.

Battle Types

Wild Pokémon Battles: Search for wild Pokémon in an area. Each area has a higher chance of spawning Pokémon of a specific type (e.g., Water in Cerulean City).

Trainer Battles: Battle NPC trainers with set Pokémon parties (relevant to the area’s type). You cannot catch their Pokémon.

Gym Battles: Fight Gym Leaders with stronger Pokémon parties. Winning earns a Gym Badge.

Elite Four: The final challenge at Indigo Plateau, featuring tough opponents.

Game Rules and Explanations

Battle Actions

Accept Challenge: Starts a Trainer, Gym, or Elite Four battle.

Attack: Your Pokémon attacks the opponent. The Pokémon with higher HP wins the exchange, but its HP is reduced by the opponent’s HP. The loser’s HP is set to 0.

Throw Pokéball: Available only in wild Pokémon battles after HP is 0. Catches the Pokémon, adding it to your party (or Collection if your party is full).

Use Potion: Restores 20 HP to your current Pokémon. Can be used at any time during a battle.

Switch Pokémon: Select the next Pokémon in your party to fight.

Run Away: Ends a wild Pokémon battle and returns you to the Area screen. Not available in Trainer, Gym, or Elite Four battles.

Game Rules and Explanations

Battle Actions Cont.

Accept Gym Badge: Claims a badge after defeating a Gym Leader.

Accept Defeat: Returns to the Area screen after a battle.

Battle Outcomes

Victory: Defeat all opponent Pokémon (their HP reaches 0).

Defeat: If all your party Pokémon reach 0 HP, it’s Game Over.

Rewards: Gold and a chance of Rare Candy wild Pokémon and varies trainer battles.

Rare Candy gives +1 EXP to a Pokémon (used outside of battle).

EXP: Pokémon gain 1 EXP per battle won.

Game Rules and Explanations

Pokémon Growth

Experience (EXP): Pokémon gain EXP from battles. At 10 EXP, a Pokémon evolves into its next form (if available) or gains bonus HP (amount TBD) if it cannot evolve. Final evolutions require 30 EXP.

Evolution: Pokémon can only evolve if they have an evolved form (tracked via an “evolvesFrom” property).

Power: A Pokémon’s strength is based on its HP. Higher HP means better performance in battles.

Poké Center

Heal Pokémon: Restores all party Pokémon to full HP.

Check Collection: View and transfer Pokémon between your party and Collection.

Leave: Returns to the Area screen.

Game Rules and Explanations

Wild Pokémon and Rarity

Use “Search for Wild Pokémon” to encounter a wild Pokémon. A random Pokémon will spawn, with a higher chance for the area’s specific type.

Rarity: Pokémon have a rarity value affecting their spawnrate. Rare Pokémon are harder to find.

Catching: Reduce a wild Pokémon’s HP to 0, then use a Pokéball to catch it.

Strategy Tips

Heal Often: Visit Poké Centers and use Potions during battle.

Catch Pokémon: Build a strong team by catching wild Pokémon.

Battle Trainers: Gain EXP to strengthen your team.

Evolve Pokémon: Use battles and Rare Candies to evolve Pokémon.
